The Itinerary

South to north — from the Neckar valley through the Black Forest, up to the Rhine Gorge, and on to Frankfurt.

Arrival & the Neckar Valley

May 16–20 — Based in Nürtingen

Day 1
Saturday, May 16

Frankfurt to Nürtingen

Land at Frankfurt, hop on a train south to your home base on the Neckar.

ICE + MEX — Frankfurt Airport → Stuttgart → Nürtingen, ~2 hrs
  • 10:00 AM arrival at Frankfurt Airport (FRA)
  • Frankfurt Flughafen Fernbahnhof — the long-distance train station is inside the airport; follow signs to "Fernbahnhof"
  • ICE to Stuttgart Hbf — ~1 hr 10 min, trains every 30 min
  • Stuttgart → Nürtingen — MEX 12/18, ~35 min (same platform level)
  • Afternoon: Check in, rest, and take a first stroll through Nürtingen's old town and along the Neckar
Day 2
Sunday, May 17

Explore Nürtingen

A relaxed day discovering your home base on the Neckar.

On foot — explore the town
  • Old Town — winding alleys and half-timbered houses that inspired the poet Hölderlin
  • Neckar Riverfront — circular walk crossing several bridges with valley views
  • St. Laurentius Church — Gothic church crowning the old town hilltop
  • Villa Rustica — Roman estate ruins on the edge of town
Day 3
Monday, May 18

Nürtingen & Wedding at Burg Hohenneuffen

Morning in town, afternoon celebrating at a castle on the Swabian Alb.

RB 65 Tälesbahn — 15 min to Neuffen
  • Morning: Explore Nürtingen — Neckar Höhenweg walk or old town stroll
  • Afternoon: Wedding at Burg Hohenneuffen — take the RB 65 to Neuffen, then taxi or walk (~45 min uphill) to the castle
  • Hohenneuffen Castle — stunning hilltop venue with panoramic views across the Swabian Alb
  • Evening: Return to Nürtingen via Neuffen (last trains run until ~11 PM; arrange taxi as backup)
Day 4
Tuesday, May 19

Stuttgart & Esslingen

The state capital and Germany's finest half-timbered town, back to back.

MEX 12 / MEX 18 — 30–44 min to Stuttgart
  • Mercedes-Benz Museum — 160 vehicles across 9 floors of automotive history
  • Schlossplatz — sweeping palace square with the Neues Schloss
  • Markthalle — stunning Art Nouveau market hall, great for lunch
  • Esslingen am Neckar — stop on the way back; 200+ half-timbered houses (oldest row in Germany, 1328), hilltop castle, and the Rathaus astronomical clock
Day 5
Wednesday, May 20

Tübingen

A fairytale university town of cobbled lanes and punt boats.

RE 6 / MEX 12 — 25 min, 49 trains/day
  • Neckarfront — the iconic row of colorful houses along the river
  • Hohentübingen Castle — hilltop castle with valley panoramas
  • Hölderlinturm — the yellow tower museum on the Neckar
  • Stocherkahn ride — punt boat on the Neckar (a Tübingen tradition)
  • Marktplatz — medieval market square with the ornate Rathaus

Black Forest & Heidelberg

May 21–22 — Heading west and north

Day 6
Thursday, May 21

Black Forest Road Trip

Rent a car and drive Germany's oldest scenic route through dark pines, waterfalls, and glacial lakes.

Rental car from Stuttgart Hbf — Sixt / Europcar
  • Pick up rental at Stuttgart Hbf (Sixt or Europcar, ~€40–60/day) — take the MEX from Nürtingen first
  • Schwarzwaldhochstraße (B500) — the legendary 60 km Black Forest High Road from Baden-Baden to Freudenstadt at 800–1,000m elevation
  • Mummelsee — glacial lake at 1,036m surrounded by dense forest, with a lakeside cafe and circular trail
  • Allerheiligen Waterfalls — cascading falls with monastery ruins at the top
  • Triberg Waterfalls — Germany's highest at 163m across 7 cascades, plus the world's biggest cuckoo clocks
  • Drop off car in Stuttgart or Heidelberg in the evening; stay overnight in Heidelberg
Day 7
Friday, May 22

Heidelberg

Romantic ruins, the oldest university, and Germany's most famous bridge.

Full day on foot — overnight in Heidelberg
  • Heidelberg Castle — iconic ruins above the Neckar; take the Bergbahn funicular up
  • Alte Brücke — 18th-century stone bridge, one of Germany's most photographed
  • Philosophenweg — Philosophers' Walk with sweeping views of the castle and old town
  • Altstadt — Germany's longest pedestrian street, cafes and bookshops
  • Heidelberg University — founded 1386, Germany's oldest
  • Evening: Train to Bacharach in the Rhine Valley (~1.5 hrs) to check in for two nights

The Rhine Gorge

May 23–24 — Based in Bacharach, UNESCO World Heritage

Day 8
Saturday, May 23

Rhine Valley — Bacharach

Half-timbered wine village beneath a hilltop castle, in the heart of the Rhine Gorge.

On foot + KD Rhine cruise
  • Bacharach old town — cobblestone lanes, half-timbered houses, Wernerkapelle gothic ruin
  • Burg Stahleck — 12th-century castle overlooking the Rhine (now a hostel, but the courtyard views are free)
  • KD Rhine cruise — hop-on hop-off boat through the UNESCO gorge past 40+ castles; the most scenic stretch is Bacharach to St. Goar
  • Lorelei Rock — the legendary 132m slate cliff at the Rhine's narrowest point, visible from the boat
  • Burg Rheinfels — vast castle ruin in St. Goar with underground tunnels (short train or boat from Bacharach)
  • Evening: Rhine wine tasting in a Bacharach Weinstube
Day 9
Sunday, May 24

Early Train & 11 AM Flight

An early departure from Bacharach to Frankfurt Airport for your morning flight.

Bacharach → Frankfurt Airport — ~1 hr 15 min
  • 11:00 AM flight — aim to arrive at Frankfurt Airport by 8:30–9:00 AM
  • ~7:30 AM train from Bacharach → Frankfurt Flughafen (Airport) — direct, ~1 hr 15 min
  • Backup: First train departs Bacharach around 5:37 AM if you prefer extra buffer
  • Alternative: Stay in Frankfurt or Mainz the night of the 23rd for a more relaxed morning
  • Book ICE Sparpreis early on bahn.de for fares from ~€18

Ticket Tips

Save money with these regional and national rail passes.

Baden-Württemberg-Ticket

€35

Unlimited travel on all regional trains, buses, and trams across the entire state for one day. Each additional traveler is just €10. Valid weekdays 9am–3am, weekends from midnight. Perfect for the Tübingen and Nürtingen-based day trips.

Deutschland-Ticket

~€49/month

Monthly pass for all regional and local transport nationwide — including the Rhine Valley trains. If you're taking 3+ day trips on regional trains, this beats buying individual day tickets.

ICE Sparpreis

from €18

Discounted advance-purchase tickets for high-speed ICE trains. Best for the Heidelberg and Frankfurt legs. Book early on bahn.de or the DB Navigator app for the lowest fares.

Rental Car (Black Forest)

~€40–60/day

Pick up and drop off at Stuttgart Hauptbahnhof — both Sixt and Europcar have desks at the station. A car is the best way to explore the Black Forest's scenic mountain roads and waterfalls at your own pace.

KD Rhine Cruise

~€15–36

Hop-on hop-off boats between Koblenz and Bingen, running daily Apr–Oct. KD celebrates its 200th anniversary in 2026. The Bacharach–St. Goar stretch passes the Lorelei and the densest cluster of castles. Free with Eurail pass.
